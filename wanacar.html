Wanacar is a car marketplace with integrated financing — but the product wasn’t solving the real problem.

Users weren’t struggling to find a car. They were struggling to understand if they could afford one.

We redefined the experience from browsing inventory to making a financial decision. The goal wasn’t just usability — it was building trust in a high-stakes moment.

Services

  • Product Design
  • Financing Experience
  • Marketplace Strategy
  • Cross-product Integration

The Challenge

Designing trust in a low-confidence financial decision.

The challenge wasn’t just improving an outdated experience — it was designing a loan flow from scratch in a market where users have low trust in financial products.

We identified three critical gaps:

  • Lack of clarity around monthly payments
  • Low trust in financing terms
  • High cognitive load during decision-making

This wasn’t a UX problem. It was a confidence problem.

Customer Journey

From buying intent to financial decision-making.

Mapping the journey revealed a key insight: users were not only trying to buy cars — many were trying to solve liquidity problems.

This shifted our perspective. The problem wasn’t just about helping users find a car — it was about helping them make a financial decision based on their reality.

  • Users needed to understand affordability early
  • Some users didn’t actually want to sell — they needed liquidity
  • Confidence increased when users felt they had control over the outcome

This insight opened a strategic opportunity: instead of treating selling and financing as separate flows, we connected them.

We introduced a cross-selling strategy between the marketplace and the car-backed loan service: “If what you need is liquidity, you don’t have to sell your car — you can use it as collateral.”

This allowed us to capture a new segment of users — people who entered the platform trying to sell their car, but were better served by accessing liquidity instead.

The impact went beyond UX. We transformed an exit point into a new acquisition channel for the loan product.

Opportunity

Competing on clarity, not inventory.

Most competitors focused on showcasing inventory — but failed at explaining the financial reality behind the purchase.

This created an opportunity: instead of competing on selection, Wanacar could compete on clarity.

In financial products, clarity is not a feature — it’s a trust mechanism.

Validation

Designing less, validating more.

We used low-fidelity prototypes to validate how much information users actually needed — not how much we could show.

Behavioral data showed users ignored information they didn’t trust. This pushed us to simplify aggressively.

Iteration

Reducing friction between intent and action.

Instead of adding features, we removed steps and simplified decisions.

Every design decision was evaluated based on one question: does this help the user move forward with confidence?

Core Experience

The loan simulator as the product core.

We reframed the experience from “apply for financing” to “understand what you can afford.”

By focusing on just three variables — down payment, term, and monthly payment — we made a complex decision understandable.

Real-time feedback gave users control, turning uncertainty into confidence.

Impact

From browsing cars to enabling financial decisions.

The redesigned experience reduced friction across the funnel and increased engagement with financing tools.

  • Higher interaction with the loan simulator
  • Increased progression into financing steps
  • New acquisition channel through cross-selling

More importantly, the product shifted from showcasing inventory to helping users make financial decisions with confidence.
